A useful wardrobe audit is not a purge. It is a pattern-finding exercise.

Instead of starting with what to remove, start with what keeps showing up. The sweater you reach for twice a week. The jeans that work with everything. The button-down that only makes sense when the weather, shoes, and mood all cooperate.

Those choices are data. They tell you where your style is already fluent.

## Three piles that actually help

Try sorting clothes into useful, unfinished, and fantasy.

Useful pieces are already earning their space. Unfinished pieces need context: tailoring, the right shoe, a better layer, or a clearer occasion. Fantasy pieces belong to a version of your life that may or may not be real.

The goal is not to own less. The goal is to understand more.
